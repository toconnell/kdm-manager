Frequently Asked Questions

Who develops this application? If you are not affiliated with Kingdom Death, where do the subscription fees go? The Manager is developed and maintained by an independent production company based in Chicago, IL called The Labor in Vain.
  Please review the "About" panel on the Manager's dashboard for more information.
What devices does the Manager support? The Manager is developed to support the current version of Chrome on Windows 10. Check the development blog for more information about supported devices.
How do I report a bug or a problem with the Manager? There are two ways to report issues.
  The fastest way is to open the left-side navigation menu, click or tap "Report an Issue or Error" and fill out a short report. This emails the maintainers of the Manager directly and includes information about you and your settlements that makes issues easier to research.
  Alternately, if you use GitHub, feel free to open issues in the Manager's public repository.

System

Why are certain items on the navigation bar and the settlement sheet greyed out and disabled? Certain features of the Manager are available to subscribers only: this includes the Gear Card Lookup, the ability to apply settlement 'macros', create multiple survivors at once, etc.
  Subscriptions of various durations can be purchased at shop.thelaborinvain.com.

Players

How do I add players to a campaign? To add players to a settlement/campaign, open the Survivor Sheet of any survivor, click on "Survivor Administration" and set the email address of the survivor to the email address of the player who you want to use it.
  Doing this does not send an email or alert to that person (for security/privacy reasons).
  Assuming that the email address belongs to a registered user of the Manager, making them the "owner" of the survivor will allow that player to access your settlement (they will see it on their dashboard) and to manage the survivor and any "public" survivors in your settlement.

Settlements

What version of Monster does the Manager currently support?  As of Friday, October 10th, 2017, the Manager supports version 1.5 of Monster as its default/base version. Lower versions are not supported.
  Beginning in 2021, all subsequent versions of Monster will be supported. An individual settlement's version can be set on the 'Admin' tab of the Settlement Sheet.
Can I configure my settlement to use other versions? Versions 1.3.1 and 1.4 are not supported by the KD:M API and there is no plan to add support for those versions.
  Versions 1.5 and later, however, are supported.
  Subscribers may change their settlement's ruleset on the "Admin" tab of the Settlement Sheet.
Why can't I create a new settlement? Non-subscriber users are limited to three (3) settlements. "Abandoned" settlements count towards this total. If you are not a subscriber to the Manager, please consider purchasing a subscription. Alternately, you may wish to remove one of your existing settlements.
  To do this, make sure that you have enabled the "Delete" button (check the "System" panel on the dashboard) and then, from the Settlement Sheet, permanently delete the settlement.
Why does my Timeline include Lantern Year 0? All settlement timelines begin with Lantern Year 0. The offical FAQ confirms that the Prologue takes place during "Lantern Year 0. When the Timeline is updated in the first Settlement Phase it becomes Lantern Year 1."
I accidentally selected a principle. How do I remove it? On the Settlement Sheet, just beneath the checkboxes for selecting principles, there is a bold, maroon-colored checkbox next to the words "Unset Principle". Click the maroon checkbox and select the principle you with to remove. The Manager remove the principle and undo any modifications that its selection may have made to your survivors.
When I return Departing survivors, does the Manager check if any have the "Tinker" ability? No. For now, the Manager only evaluates whether survivors are alive or dead when returned and uses that information to determine how many Endeavors to add. In the future, this may change, but for now, this is how it works.
My settlement disappeared! Settlements created by users who do not subscribe to the Manager are removed after they reach 180 days of age (the settlements, not the users). If you believe that your settlement was removed in error, please use the "Report an Issue or Error" controls on the left-side menu to ask for help.

Strain System

How do I add Strain Fighting Arts? When creating a new campaign (or updating an old one), add one of the Echoes of Death or, if you have any of the Echoes of Death expansions in your KD Collection, enable the "Fighting Arts & Disorders" from your collection.
  At that point, the appropriate Strain Fighting Arts will be available in the Fighting Arts drop-down on Survivor Sheets for that campaign.
  Please note that all Strain Fighting Arts will be available, even if their Strain Milestone has not been recorded on the Settlement Sheet.
Will the Manager assign locked Strain Fighting Arts if I choose a random Fighting Art on the Survivor Sheet?  No. Selecting the '* Random Fighting Art' option from the drop-down will pick a random Fighting Art from a list that does not include Strain Fighting Arts that have not yet been recorded on the Settlement Sheet.
Does the Manager enforce the five card limit on Strain Fighting Arts?  No. As mentioned above, your settlement's Fighting Arts drop-down will always let you pick any Strain Fighting Art from your enabled expansion content.

Survivors

What is the difference between "New" and "Newborn" survivors? The Manager distinguishes between "new" and "newborn" survivors: survivors created without parents are considered "new", while survivors created with parents are considered "newborn."
  Bonuses and effects that apply to "newborn" survivors will be automatically applied to them at "birth" (i.e. creation), but those bonuses will not be applied at creation time to survivors who are merely "new".
How do I create survivors with parents? Advance the Timeline to Lantern Year 1 or later. Survivors added to the settlement in LY 0 may not have parents. Such survivors are considered "new" and are not subject to "newborn" benefits inherent to campaign or expansion content.
Why can't I add the Lovelorn Rock to my gear grid? The Rock is not part of the core game. Rather, it is part of the Vignettes of Death: White Gigalion and, if you want to equip it (or add it to settlement storage for some reason), you must first add that expansion content to your settlement.
